# SparkLocal Blog Content Plan — 30 Posts

**Goal:** Build topical authority, capture informational search traffic, drive users to directory + builder
**Target:** 30 posts by end of May 2026
**Cadence:** 8-10 in March (heavy push), then 3-4/month

---

## Content Principles

1. **Target long-tail keywords** — SparkLocal is a new, low-DA domain. We can't outrank NerdWallet for "small business grants." We CAN rank for "small business grants for veterans in Texas" or "how to find a business mentor near me."

2. **Link to directory pages** — Every post must link to 3-5 relevant directory pages (category pages, city hubs, state guides, individual listings). This builds internal link equity and drives traffic to programmatic pages.

3. **Link to the builder** — Every post should have at least one CTA to "Start Building Your Business Plan" linking to /builder. This is the conversion path.

4. **Be specific, not generic** — Don't write "consider finding a coworking space." Write "SparkLocal lists 2,890 coworking spaces across 547 cities — find one near you." Use real numbers from the directory.

5. **Differentiate from ChatGPT** — Include real data points, real resource names, links to actual programs. Things an AI chatbot can't easily provide.

6. **1,500-2,500 words per post** — Long enough to rank, short enough to write efficiently.

## Content Template for Claude Code

Each blog post should be a markdown file in content/blog/ with:
- Frontmatter: title, slug, description, date, tags, author
- 1,500-2,500 words of original content
- H2 and H3 subheadings (structured for featured snippets)
- Internal links as described above
- No fluff — every paragraph should provide specific, actionable information
- Include real data where possible (directory stats, grant amounts, deadlines)
- End with a clear CTA section

Use this prompt template for Claude Code:
```
Write a blog post for SparkLocal (sparklocal.co), a business resource directory with 3,866 listings across 547 US cities and 12 categories (coworking, grants, accelerators, SBA, business attorneys, accountants, marketing agencies, business insurance, chambers of commerce, virtual offices, commercial real estate, business consultants).

Title: [TITLE]
Target keyword: [KEYWORD]
Word count: [WORD COUNT]

Requirements:
- Write for entrepreneurs who are just getting started. Practical, specific, actionable.
- Include real data points and specific program names where relevant.
- DO NOT mention SparkLocal in the body text more than twice. Let the internal links do the work.
- Include 5-7 H2 subheadings structured as questions or action phrases.
- End with a "Next Steps" section that naturally links to SparkLocal directory pages.
- Tone: Helpful, direct, no fluff. Like a knowledgeable friend who happens to know a lot about business.

Internal links to include:
[LIST SPECIFIC DIRECTORY LINKS]
```
